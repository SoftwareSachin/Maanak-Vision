The issue isn't the prompt length — it's that you're not giving the AI a **visual reference frame** it can actually follow. Here's what to add/change:

---

**The core fix — add this block at the very top of your prompt:**

---

**DESIGN REFERENCE — COPY THESE APPS, NOT AI TEMPLATES:**

Study and replicate the exact visual decisions made by these specific Indian product teams:

* **Swiggy** — how they use orange on dark cards, dense order-status rows, bottom sheet interactions, large restaurant cards with real photography
* **PhonePe / Google Pay India** — how transaction history looks: tight rows, left icon + center label + right amount, no wasted space
* **CRED** — dark background, white typography hierarchy, large bold numbers, receipt-style breakdowns
* **Groww** — how they show red/green stock data: color-coded text inline, no separate legend, numbers are the UI
* **Juspay / Razorpay dashboards** — dense data tables that still feel clean, status badges (pill-shaped, color-filled, not outlined)

**What these apps have in common that AI never generates by default:**
* Real photography or real icons — never flat vector illustrations of robots or circuits
* Rows of data, not cards of data — information is horizontal and dense, not spread across floating cards
* Color is functional, not decorative — red means failed transaction, green means success, that's it
* No hero sections, no onboarding illustrations of cartoon factory workers
* Bottom sheets instead of new screens for secondary actions
* The empty state is a simple message, not an illustrated mascot

---

**EXPLICIT BANS — tell the AI what NOT to do:**

Do NOT generate any of the following:
* Gradient backgrounds (purple-to-blue, yellow-to-orange, any gradient)
* Glassmorphism (frosted card effects, blur behind panels)
* Lottie / animated illustrations of any kind
* Rounded blob shapes or organic decorative shapes
* An "AI brain" icon, neural network graphic, or circuit board visual
* A mascot or illustrated character representing the app
* Onboarding slides with full-page illustrations
* Floating 3D elements or isometric graphics
* Any icon that is abstract — every icon must map to a real physical object a factory worker would recognize
* Section headers styled like marketing copy ("Empower your quality journey")
* Light mode with white cards on white background

---

**Tell it exactly what the screens LOOK like — not what they DO:**

Replace vague UX descriptions with layout instructions like these:

> **Inspection Screen:** Black background. Full-bleed camera feed takes 70% of screen height. Overlaid on the feed: a white corner-bracket reticle (like a camera focus box, not a glowing AI overlay). Bottom 30%: a dark charcoal (#1C1C1E) panel pinned to the bottom. Inside that panel: one large pill-shaped button (Safety Yellow, full width, 60dp height, label "SCAN PART" in Rajdhani Bold 18sp). Above the button: a single status row — left-aligned icon + status text in 14sp. No decorative elements.

> **Result Screen:** Full-screen color flash (green #22C55E or red #EF4444) for 400ms, then resolves to result card. Card has: large PASS / FAIL label in 48sp bold at top center, defect type below in 16sp, thumbnail of scanned part on the right, timestamp + batch number in 12sp muted text at bottom. One action button: "LOG & NEXT" in yellow. Nothing else on screen.

> **Batch History Screen:** Mimics a WhatsApp chat list or Swiggy order history. Each row: left — part thumbnail (40x40dp square, not rounded), center — part name (16sp medium) + batch ID (12sp muted), right — PASS/FAIL pill badge (green or red filled, white text 12sp). Rows separated by a 0.5px border. No cards, no shadows, no elevation.

---

**One final instruction to paste at the very end of your prompt:**

> Before generating any UI, ask yourself: "Would a Swiggy designer approve this screen?" If the answer is no — if there is a gradient, an illustration, an abstract icon, or a decorative element that serves no functional purpose — remove it. The target aesthetic is: dark, dense, functional, Indian. It should look like it was built by a 10-person product team in Koramangala, not generated by an AI.

---

That's the fix. The prompt you had was good on *what to build* — it just never told the AI *what real Indian apps actually look like at a pixel level*, so it fell back on its default "AI product app" aesthetic. These additions give it a concrete visual frame to copy from.
